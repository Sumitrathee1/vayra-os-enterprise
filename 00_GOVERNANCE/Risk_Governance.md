# Purpose

This document defines the Risk Governance framework for VAYRA OS Enterprise.

It establishes a standardized approach for identifying, assessing, mitigating, monitoring, and reviewing risks that may affect business operations, AI systems, projects, finances, clients, security, and organizational growth.

Risk Governance enables informed decision-making while protecting the long-term sustainability of VAYRA Atelier.

---

# Mission

To proactively identify and manage risks in a structured, transparent, and consistent manner that supports business continuity, responsible AI adoption, and operational excellence.

---

# Objectives

The Risk Governance framework aims to:

- Identify potential risks early.
- Standardize risk assessment.
- Prioritize risks based on impact and likelihood.
- Define mitigation strategies.
- Assign ownership for every significant risk.
- Monitor risk throughout its lifecycle.
- Promote continuous improvement.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to risks involving:

- Strategic Planning
- Business Operations
- Client Projects
- AI Systems
- Enterprise Architecture
- Documentation
- Financial Management
- Human Resources
- Technology
- Information Security
- Legal and Compliance
- Vendor Relationships

---

# Risk Governance Principles

## Proactive

Identify risks before they become issues.

---

## Business Focused

Evaluate risks based on their impact on business objectives.

---

## Data-Driven

Use available evidence, metrics, and historical information whenever possible.

---

## Accountability

Every significant risk must have an assigned owner.

---

## Transparency

Risk decisions and mitigation plans should be documented and reviewable.

---

## Continuous Monitoring

Risks should be reviewed regularly throughout their lifecycle.

# Risk Assessment

Every identified risk should be evaluated using:

- Probability
- Business Impact
- Financial Impact
- Client Impact
- Operational Impact
- Technical Impact
- Reputation Impact
- Recovery Complexity

---

# Risk Levels

## Low Risk

Minor impact with limited disruption.

Review periodically.

---

## Medium Risk

Noticeable impact requiring mitigation planning.

Monitor regularly.

---

## High Risk

Major impact requiring executive attention and immediate mitigation.

---

## Critical Risk

Threatens business continuity or enterprise stability.

Requires immediate executive action.

---

# Risk Response Strategies

Depending on the assessment, risks may be handled by:

- Avoiding the risk.
- Reducing the likelihood.
- Reducing the impact.
- Transferring the risk.
- Accepting the risk with documented justification.

---

# Risk Ownership

Each significant risk should have:

- Risk Owner
- Reviewer
- Decision Authority
- Mitigation Owner
- Review Schedule

---

# Risk Monitoring

Risk reviews should include:

- Current status
- Changes in likelihood
- Changes in impact
- Mitigation effectiveness
- Emerging risks
- Required actions

---

# Risk Register

Each recorded risk should include:

- Risk ID
- Title
- Description
- Category
- Owner
- Probability
- Impact
- Overall Rating
- Mitigation Plan
- Review Date
- Current Status

---

# Escalation

Immediate escalation is required when:

- Risk becomes Critical.
- Multiple departments are affected.
- Client commitments are threatened.
- Financial exposure exceeds approved limits.
- AI systems produce unsafe or unreliable outputs.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Lessons learned from incidents should be used to:

- Improve workflows.
- Strengthen controls.
- Update governance documents.
- Enhance AI behavior.
- Refine operational processes.

---

# Success Indicators

Risk Governance is successful when:

- Risks are identified early.
- Mitigation plans are implemented.
- Business disruption is minimized.
- Decision-makers have visibility into key risks.
- Organizational resilience improves over time.
