# Purpose

This document defines the governance framework for Artificial Intelligence within VAYRA OS Enterprise.

It establishes the policies, responsibilities, controls, lifecycle, authority boundaries, monitoring mechanisms, and ethical standards that govern the design, deployment, operation, and continuous improvement of AI agents across VAYRA Atelier.

This framework ensures that AI systems remain aligned with business objectives while operating safely, transparently, responsibly, and consistently.

---

# Mission

To build an enterprise AI ecosystem that is secure, trustworthy, scalable, accountable, and capable of supporting business growth through responsible AI governance.

---

# Objectives

The AI Governance framework aims to:

- Standardize AI operations.
- Define AI responsibilities and authority.
- Ensure responsible AI behavior.
- Protect enterprise knowledge.
- Govern AI lifecycle management.
- Establish monitoring and oversight.
- Promote transparency and accountability.
- Support continuous learning and improvement.

---

# Scope

This framework applies to:

- Executive AI Agents
- Department AI Agents
- Specialized AI Agents
- AI Workflows
- AI Memory Systems
- AI Automation
- Prompt Libraries
- Knowledge Bases
- Context Engines
- Future AI Capabilities

---

# AI Governance Principles

## Business Alignment

Every AI capability must support the strategic objectives of VAYRA Atelier.

---

## Human Oversight

Critical business, financial, legal, architectural, and strategic decisions require appropriate human review and approval.

---

## Accountability

Every AI agent must have:

- Defined Owner
- Defined Purpose
- Defined Authority
- Defined Responsibilities

---

## Transparency

AI reasoning, recommendations, assumptions, and limitations should be documented whenever appropriate.

---

## Responsible Operation

AI agents must:

- Operate ethically.
- Avoid misleading information.
- Respect confidentiality.
- Stay within their approved authority.

---

## Continuous Improvement

AI capabilities should evolve through feedback, governance reviews, lessons learned, and controlled updates.

---

# AI Lifecycle

Every AI agent follows this lifecycle:

1. Business Need Identified
2. Requirements Defined
3. Design
4. Development
5. Testing
6. Approval
7. Deployment
8. Monitoring
9. Improvement
10. Retirement (when applicable)

# Authority Management

Each AI agent must have documented:

- Purpose
- Responsibilities
- Decision Authority
- Escalation Rules
- Collaboration Rules
- Reporting Structure

Authority must never exceed documented limits.

---

# Memory Governance

AI memory should be:

- Structured
- Relevant
- Version Controlled
- Secure
- Reviewed
- Continuously Updated

Memory retention policies should prevent duplication, outdated information, and unauthorized access.

---

# Prompt Governance

Prompts should be:

- Standardized
- Version Controlled
- Tested
- Documented
- Reviewed
- Reusable

Major prompt updates should follow Change Management procedures.

---

# AI Collaboration

AI agents should:

- Share context responsibly.
- Reuse approved knowledge.
- Escalate when necessary.
- Avoid conflicting recommendations.
- Respect defined responsibilities.

---

# Performance Monitoring

AI performance should be evaluated using:

- Accuracy
- Consistency
- Response Quality
- Business Value
- User Satisfaction
- Compliance
- Productivity
- Reliability

---

# Risk Management

AI risks include:

- Hallucinations
- Context Loss
- Outdated Knowledge
- Prompt Failures
- Unauthorized Access
- Inconsistent Decisions
- Automation Errors

These risks should be assessed and managed in accordance with Risk_Governance.md.

---

# Security Requirements

AI systems must:

- Follow Security_Governance.md.
- Protect confidential information.
- Restrict access based on roles.
- Prevent unauthorized disclosure.
- Operate within approved environments.

# Review Cycle

AI Governance should be reviewed:

- Annually
- After major AI upgrades
- Following significant incidents

# Success Indicators

AI Governance is successful when:

- AI supports business objectives.
- AI decisions are accountable.
- AI operates securely.
- AI remains within defined authority.
- AI continuously improves.
- Enterprise trust in AI increases.
