# Purpose

This document defines the decision-making framework for VAYRA OS Enterprise.

It establishes how strategic, operational, financial, technical, and AI-related decisions are initiated, evaluated, approved, implemented, and reviewed to ensure consistency, accountability, and alignment with the organization's objectives.

---

# Mission

To provide a structured, transparent, and repeatable decision-making process that enables effective governance while supporting business growth, operational excellence, and responsible AI adoption.

---

# Objectives

The Decision Governance framework aims to:

- Standardize decision-making processes.
- Define decision ownership.
- Clarify approval authority.
- Promote evidence-based decisions.
- Reduce decision-related risks.
- Ensure organizational accountability.
- Improve collaboration across departments.
- Maintain traceable decision records.

---

# Decision Principles

Every decision should be:

## Business Aligned

Support the long-term goals of VAYRA Atelier.

---

## Evidence-Based

Use verified data, metrics, and documented knowledge whenever possible.

---

## Transparent

Clearly document the reasoning, assumptions, and expected outcomes.

---

## Accountable

Assign a responsible owner for every decision.

---

## Timely

Balance thorough evaluation with efficient execution.

---

## Risk Aware

Assess potential risks before implementation.

---

## Client Focused

Consider the impact on clients and project quality.

# Decision Lifecycle

Every major decision follows this sequence:

1. Identify the Issue or Opportunity
2. Define the Objective
3. Gather Relevant Information
4. Consult Stakeholders
5. Evaluate Alternatives
6. Assess Risks
7. Select the Preferred Option
8. Obtain Required Approval
9. Implement the Decision
10. Monitor Outcomes
11. Capture Lessons Learned
12. Update Organizational Knowledge

---

# Decision Criteria

Before approval, evaluate:

- Strategic Alignment
- Business Value
- Financial Impact
- Client Impact
- Operational Feasibility
- Technical Feasibility
- Resource Availability
- Risk Level
- Legal or Compliance Considerations
- Long-Term Sustainability

---

# Decision Authority

Every decision must have:

- Decision Owner
- Reviewer (if required)
- Approver
- Implementation Owner

Roles_And_Authority.md

# Documentation Requirements

Significant decisions should include:

- Decision Title
- Background
- Objective
- Options Considered
- Selected Option
- Justification
- Risks
- Approval Details
- Expected Outcomes
- Review Date

---

# Escalation

Escalate decisions when:

- Authority limits are exceeded.
- Risks are high.
- Multiple departments are affected.
- Significant financial investment is required.
- AI behavior or architecture changes are proposed.

---

# Monitoring

After implementation:

- Track KPIs.
- Compare expected vs actual outcomes.
- Review stakeholder feedback.
- Record lessons learned.
- Update workflows if necessary.

---

# Success Indicators

Decision Governance is effective when:

- Decisions are timely.
- Approvals are documented.
- Responsibilities are clear.
- Risks are managed.
- Outcomes align with business goals.
- Knowledge is continuously improved.
