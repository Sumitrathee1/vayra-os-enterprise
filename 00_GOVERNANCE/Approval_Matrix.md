# Purpose

This document defines the approval authority for decisions, projects, financial commitments, AI systems, documentation, and operational activities within VAYRA OS Enterprise.

The Approval Matrix ensures that every significant activity is reviewed and approved by the appropriate authority before implementation.

---

# Mission

To establish a clear, transparent, and standardized approval structure that promotes accountability, reduces risk, and supports efficient decision-making across the organization.

---

# Objectives

The Approval Matrix aims to:

- Define approval responsibilities.
- Eliminate ambiguity in decision authority.
- Standardize approval workflows.
- Support governance compliance.
- Protect business interests.
- Improve operational efficiency.
- Reduce unauthorized decisions.

---

# Approval Principles

## Appropriate Authority

Every approval must be granted by the lowest authority level capable of making the decision while respecting governance policies.

---

## Accountability

Every approval must identify:

- Request Owner
- Reviewer (if applicable)
- Approver
- Implementation Owner

---

## Traceability

Approvals should be documented and retained according to enterprise standards.

---

## Risk-Based Approval

Higher-risk activities require higher levels of approval.

# Financial Approval Thresholds

| Amount | Approval Authority |
|---------|-------------------|
| Up to ₹10,000 | Department Head |
| ₹10,001 – ₹50,000 | COO |

# AI Approval Requirements

The following always require CEO approval:

- Creation of Executive AI Agents
- AI Governance changes
- Enterprise Memory changes
- Context Engine changes
- Automation affecting multiple departments
- AI authority modifications

# Emergency Approvals

For urgent situations:

1. Immediate action may be taken by the responsible executive.
2. CEO must be informed as soon as practical.
3. Formal documentation must be completed after implementation.
4. A post-implementation review is mandatory.

---

# Approval Records

Each approval should record:

- Approval ID
- Request Title
- Date
- Request Owner
- Reviewer
- Approver
- Decision
- Conditions (if any)
- Implementation Date
- Review Date

---

# Exceptions

Any exception to this Approval Matrix must:

- Be documented.
- Include business justification.
- Receive CEO approval.
- Be recorded for audit purposes.

---

# Success Indicators

The Approval Matrix is successful when:

- Approval responsibilities are clearly understood.
- Unauthorized decisions are minimized.
- Approvals are completed efficiently.
- Decision records remain complete and traceable.
- Governance compliance is maintained.
